Gyudon

What Is It?

Gyudon (Japanese Beef Bowl) "The ultimate Japanese comfort classic: paper-thin savory beef and caramelized onions simmered in a sweet-salty dashi glaze, served over a steaming bed of fluffy rice.

Gyudon is a beloved staple of Japanese "fast-casual" dining, prized for its perfect balance of simplicity and deep umami flavor. The magic lies in the simmering liquid—a delicate blend of dashi, soy sauce, and mirin—which transforms humble yellow onions into tender, translucent ribbons of sweetness. Unlike a heavy stew, the beef is sliced paper-thin so it cooks in seconds, absorbing the broth while remaining incredibly light and tender.

It is the quintessential "quick-build" meal. While the dish is satisfying on its own, it truly comes alive with its traditional garnishes: the sharp, acidic crunch of red pickled ginger (beni shoga) and the richness of a silken egg yolk. Each bowl is a customizable experience, designed to be assembled in minutes but savored slowly.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb very thiny sliced beef (ribeye or sirloin)
  • 1 medium yellow onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 cups steamed Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1/2 cup dashi
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • pickled red ginger
  • sliced green onions
  • raw egg yolk or soft boiled egg (optional)

Steps-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sauce:In a medium skillet or pot, combine the dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and sake. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Simmer the Onions:Add the sliced onions to the skillet. Cover with a lid and simmer for about 3–5 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent.
  3. Cook the Beef:Add the thinly sliced beef to the skillet. Use chopsticks or a fork to separate the slices so they cook evenly. Simmer for just 2–3 minutes until the beef is no longer pink. (Don't overcook it, or the thin slices will get tough!)
  4. Skim the Surface:Use a spoon to skim off any foam or "scum" that rises to the top of the liquid to keep the sauce clean and bright.
  5. Assemble the Bowl:Fill a deep bowl with hot steamed rice. Pour the beef, onions, and a generous amount of the simmering liquid over the rice. The rice will soak up all that savory flavor.
  6. Garnish:Top with a pile of pickled ginger, a sprinkle of green onions, and an egg if you’re feeling extra fancy.
