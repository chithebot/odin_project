Tonkatsu Ramen

What Is It?

A masterclass in balance and depth, this bowl brings the heart of Japanese comfort food to your kitchen through a rich, multi-layered broth and springy, artisan-style noodles.

Ramen is far more than just a soup; it is an experience of umami. The foundation is a deeply savory broth, simmered for hours to extract every layer of flavor from aromatics and marrow. This is paired with "Kansui" (alkaline) wheat noodles, which provide a signature snap and golden hue that holds up against the heat of the soup.

Each bowl is a customizable canvas, traditionally crowned with tender, melt-in-your-mouth braised pork belly, a jammy marinated soft-boiled egg, and fresh garnishes like scallions and nori. Whether you prefer the light, salt-based clarity of Shio or the heavy, creamy indulgence of Tonkotsu, this recipe provides the blueprint for a restaurant-quality bowl at home.

Steps-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cleaning: Blanch the bones in boiling water for about 10-20 minutes to remove impurities
  2. Simmering: Drain the water, rinse the bones, and then simmer them in fresh water with aromatics (onion, garlic, ginger, and kombu).
  3. Time: For a light broth, simmer for 4-6 hours. For a rich, creamy Tonkatsu, you'll need to boil the bones aggressively for 12-18 hours to fully emulsify the fats and collagen.
  4. Shoyu: Combine soy sauce, mirin, and sake to make the Shoyu.
  5. Simmer: Bring the Shoyu mixture to a simmer and then set aside.
  6. Chashu Braise a pork belly in a mixture of soy sauce, sugar, ginger, and garlic for several hours until tender.
  7. Marinated Eggs: Soft-boil eggs for about 6 minutes and put them in ice water. Then marinate them in a mixture of soy sauce and mirin for at least 4 hours.
  8. Vegetables: Prepare menma and chopped onions.
  9. Boil: Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions.
  10. Drain: Drain them thoroughly to avoid diluting the broth.
  11. Tare: Add 1-2 tablespoons of the Shoyu to each bowl.
  12. Broth: Pour the hot broth over the tare and stire to combine.
  13. Noodles: Gently place the noodles in the broth.
  14. Toppings: Neatly arrange your chashu, eggs, and vegetables on top.
