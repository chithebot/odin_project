Ramen

Tonkatsu ramen is a soul-warming Japanese noodle soup defined by its signature creamy, opaque pork bone broth. This rich base is achieved by boiling pork marrow and fats for several hours until the collagen breaks down into a silky, savory emulsion.

A typical bowl is served with thin, snappy wheat noodles and topped with tender slices of braised pork belly (chashu), green onions, and often a soft-boiled marinated egg. Because of its heavy, umami-forward profile, it is widely considered one of the most indulgent and satisfying styles of ramen.
